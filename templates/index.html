Numerical Method for Approximating Solutions of Ordinary Differential Equations

Description

Euler's Method is a numerical technique for approximating solutions to first-order differential equations. It uses a known starting point and slope to estimate future values step by step. Smaller step sizes improve accuracy. This method is widely applied in engineering, physics, mathematics, and simulations for solving complex real-world problems.

Formula

\[ y_{n+1}=y_n+h f(x_n,y_n) \]

Where:

\(y_n\) = current value of y

\(y_{n+1}\) = next approximated value of y

\(x_n\) = current x value

h = step size or interval length

\(f(x_n,y_n)\) = slope function \(dy/dx\)

Worked Example 1

Given: \( dy/dx=x+y \)

\( y(0)=1,\ h=0.1 \)

Solutions:

Step 1:

\( y_1=1+0.1(1)=1.1 \)

Step 2:

\( y_2=1.1+0.1(1.2)=1.22 \)

Step 3:

\( y_3=1.22+0.1(1.42)=1.362 \)

Worked Example 2

Given: \( dy/dx=x-y \)

\( y(0)=2,\ h=0.2 \)

Solutions:

Step 1:

\( y_1=2+0.2(-2)=1.6 \)

Step 2:

\( y_2=1.6+0.2(-1.4)=1.32 \)

Step 3:

\( y_3=1.32+0.2(-0.92)=1.136 \)

How to write your equation correctly

Rules to follow:

  • Use * for multiplication
    Write 2*y ❌ NOT 2y
  • Use ^ for exponents
    Write x^2 ❌ NOT x2
  • Use + and - normally
  • Allowed variables: x, y
  • Allowed constants: pi, e
  • Allowed functions: sin(), cos(), tan(), sqrt(), log(), ln(), exp(), abs()

✅ Correct Examples:

  • 2*x + y
  • x - 3*y
  • x^2 + 5*y
  • sin(x) + 2*cos(y)
  • sqrt(x) - y/2

❌ Wrong Examples (will cause error):

  • 2x + y
  • x - 3y
  • x2 + 5y
  • sin x + y
